Curriculum

The Reach curriculum is uniquely designed to meet the developmental milestones of young children, combining play-based activities with structured learning. This approach supports cognitive, social, and emotional growth. The curriculum encompasses key areas such as language acquisition, early numeracy, physical development, and the arts. By emphasizing hands-on, experiential learning, we help children to engage deeply with their environment and build a robust foundation for their future education, setting them up for success in their subsequent academic pursuits.

Extracurricular Activities

Extracurricular activities are integral to the holistic development approach of the Reach program. We offer a diverse array of activities such as arts and crafts, music and storytelling, which are designed to spark creativity and encourage self-expression. These activities not only provide a break from traditional learning but also help children develop essential social skills, confidence, and teamwork. By participating in extracurricular activities, children can discover new interests and talents in a supportive and enriching environment.

Sports & Plays

Physical activity is a cornerstone of the Reach program, promoting overall health and development. Our well-equipped play areas and structured sports activities ensure that children remain active and engaged. Through organized play and sports, children enhance their physical fitness, motor skills, and teamwork abilities. We believe in the power of play as a fundamental aspect of learning, helping children to build resilience, solve problems, and interact positively with their peers. Our staff ensures that all activities are conducted safely and enjoyably, fostering a love for active living from an early age.
